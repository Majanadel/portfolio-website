Nalu

  • Role: UX Designer
  • Tools used: Sketch, Balsamic, Adobe XD, Usability Hub
  • Timeline: 8 Months

Objective

This project aims to provide beautifully displayed and easy-to-understand wind, wave, weather reports, and statistics for sailors, surfers, kite surfers, divers, and other water sports enthusiasts. People will be able to use this app for a quick 2–3-minute session to check weather conditions before heading to the water or for more extended periods when searching for new locations, people, messaging, etc.

Design Process

Understand

Problem Statement

The user needs an app that will show complex weather data, easy to read by different water enthusiasts' levels, allowing them to practice their activities safely.

Possible Solutions

Nalu should provide detailed weather reports on the forecast, tides, waves, water temperature, etc., for different activities. Data needs to be visually engaging to use colorful maps, tide changes, wind strength, etc. There should be a way for a user to find an in-depth explanation of all the app's weather conditions. It should contain a way for water enthusiasts to connect and report to each other about different hazards, give advice, connect and learn together.

Competitor analysis

I chose to compare two very popular apps for water enthusiasts, Windy and Wisuki. I used SWOT analysis to record Strengths, Weaknesses, Opportunities, and Threats of these two competitors.

Observe

User Interviews

I chose User Interviews because they will be the most valuable research method to get the information I need. Talking to people and learning about their experiences helped me to learn about their needs and goals. I tried to find people who fit in my User profile for Nalu app, water sports enthusiasts, either as a hobby or a way of life.

Interview Goals

  • To find out from different water enthusiasts how much are weather apps important to practice their sports safely and efficiently
  • What kind of apps do they use for their sports.
  • What are some of the features Users would find to be useful for the new weather app.
  • How much is social part in the app important for them to connect with other people who are practicing same sport.

Interview Findings

  • I learned that my interview participants already have their favorite weather apps. One of them is the Windy app that I recognized as a main competitor in competitive research
  • I learned that some of the participants use different apps for different tasks. For example to check the weather, to connect with other people, to shop for their equipment, to log their training
  • Participants named a lot of similar features that they are looking for in the new app

Affinity Mapping

I collected findings from the interviews about behaviors, needs, and frustrations in the affinity map.

Mid Fidelity Wireframes

Using Balsamiq, I gave my app some more life by adding details. This prototype has limited functionality, but it does have some clickable areas for interactions.

High Fidelity Wireframes

I created this part in Adobe XD. The app is in the advanced stages of the design process now, and I will do the first usability testing with it.

Usability Testing

I recruited 6 participants who tested the functionality of the Nalu app. Testing was done mostly remotely because of the situation with the Covid19 pandemic. They had five tasks and around 25 minutes for testing. I analyzed all audio and video data from the testing and created an affinity map. The map helped me see all the errors, observations, positive and negative quotes and transfer them into the Rainbow spreadsheet.

Iterations

Findings in the rainbow spreadsheet are helping me identify what works and where do I need to improve Nalu. Here, we can see how Nalu app’s design evolved based on usability testing, peer reviews, accessibility testing, emotional, and gestalt design principles.

Final Product

Nalu is an app where a user can check the weather, get the forecast for days ahead, check the specific location for a particular sport, connect with other water sports lovers, discover and learn about new places, get warning notifications, and much more.

Takeaways

This project was my first full UX project. It came with many challenges, but I can say that I learned a lot in the end. The most significant learning was not to get too attached to some design because it can change tomorrow. You are never actually happy, and iterations happen all the time.
